Transposition Cipher

Unlike the ciphers we have looked at so far where we replace each letter with a different letter or symbol to produce the ciphertext, in the Transposition Cipher, the letters are just scrambled around.

An example of a simple Transposition Cipher would be just reversing the plaintext either as a whole sentence or reversing each word. 

For example:

Ciphertext → gninnigeb eht ta nigeb.
Plaintext → Begin at the beginning.
