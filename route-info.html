Route Cipher

The Route Cipher is another transposition cipher. For this cipher, we arrange the text we want to encrypt in a grid and read it in a predefined pattern.

Fun fact: the Union Route Cipher, which was used by the Union forces during the American Civil War is a variation of the Route Cipher. Instead of transposing letters by the given route, whole words were moved around, sometimes adding whole null words to the ciphertext to help obscure meaning.
